Why switch from static to video?

Snapchat is a mobile‑first, full‑screen platform where users are accustomed to vertical, immersive content. Static images often get scrolled past, while video captures attention, tells a story, and drives action.

  • 📈 +30% higher CTR on video vs. static (Snapchat internal data)
  • 🎬 2x higher conversion rates for video‑first campaigns
  • 🧠 Better brand recall — moving visuals leave a lasting impression

💡 Key insight

Video ads on Snapchat don’t need to be high‑budget productions. Simple user‑generated style, quick cuts, and authentic storytelling often outperform polished corporate videos.

Best practices for high‑performing video ads

🎯 First 3 seconds are everything

Hook viewers immediately with movement, text, or a compelling visual. Snapchat users scroll fast – grab attention before they swipe away.

🔊 Design for sound‑off

85% of Snapchat videos are watched without sound. Use captions, text overlays, and visual storytelling to convey your message.

📱 Vertical, full‑screen

Always use 9:16 vertical format. Horizontal videos get cropped and perform poorly.

✨ Show, don’t just tell

Demonstrate your product in action. User‑generated content, unboxing, or quick tutorials work well.

🔄 Rotate creatives frequently

Even video ads suffer from fatigue. Refresh every 2–3 weeks to maintain performance.

📊 Include a clear CTA

Use Snapchat’s swipe‑up or button overlays. Tell viewers exactly what to do next.

A/B testing: more than one creative per ad set

Snapchat Ads Manager allows you to include multiple creatives (images, videos) within a single ad set. The delivery system will automatically optimize toward the best‑performing creative. However, to run a controlled test, follow this structure:

MethodHow to set upWhat to measure
Dynamic creative testing
(one ad set, multiple videos)		 Upload 2–5 video variations in the same ad set. Let Snapchat’s algorithm serve the best one over time. CTR, conversion rate, frequency, cost per result
Split test (A/B experiment)
two separate ad sets		 Create two ad sets with identical targeting and budget; one with static images, one with video. Use the built‑in A/B testing tool. Lift in CTR, CPA, ROAS
Holdout test Run a campaign with video only, compare to a control group that sees static ads (or no ads) to measure incrementality. Incremental conversions, brand lift
🧪 Pro tip: Always test one variable at a time (e.g., video length, opening hook, or caption style) to identify exactly what drives improvement.
5

Step‑by‑step transition plan

  1. Audit your current static ads – Identify top‑performing images (CTR, conversion, creative concept).
  2. Create 3‑5 video variations based on the same winning concepts. Keep one variable different per variation.
  3. Duplicate your current ad set or create a new campaign with the same targeting and budget.
  4. Add all video variations to a single ad set (dynamic testing).
  5. Run the test for at least 7–10 days or until each video gets ~5000 impressions to reach statistical significance.
  6. Analyze results – Compare CTR, conversion rate, CPA, and frequency between the original static ad set and the video ad set.
  7. Scale the winning video and iterate with new variations.

📌 Don’t pause the static campaign immediately – keep it running as a baseline while the test is active.

Even a simple, low‑production video can significantly outperform static ads when it fits the platform and speaks to the audience authentically.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many video variations should I test at once?

Start with 3–5 variations. Too many may dilute impressions before the algorithm can decide. Ensure each gets at least 5,000 impressions for reliable data.

What if I don’t have video production resources?

You can repurpose user‑generated content, testimonials, product demos shot on a smartphone, or use Snapchat’s Creative Kit to create simple animated text‑over‑image videos.

Should I use audio or not?

Audio can enhance engagement, but always design with sound‑off in mind. Use captions and strong visuals to convey the message regardless.

How long should I run the A/B test?

Run for at least 7–10 days to account for daily fluctuations and reach statistical significance. Use Snapchat’s A/B testing tool to automatically declare a winner when confidence is reached.
